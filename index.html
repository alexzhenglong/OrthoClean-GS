Abstract

Generating artifact-free digital orthophoto maps (DOMs) in dynamic urban environments remains a critical challenge in UAV photogrammetry. Although 3D Gaussian Splatting (3DGS) enables highly efficient scene reconstruction, it lacks robustness against transient objects, frequently causing severe ghosting artifacts and geometric distortions. To address this, we propose a dynamic-aware neural Gaussian framework (DA-3DGS) designed to explicitly decouple and eliminate moving vehicles from the reconstruction pipeline. Specifically, we integrate zero-shot 2D semantic priors with a rigorous 3D-2D projection mechanism to accurately localize view-dependent dynamic regions across multi-view imagery. Based on this spatial correspondence, a learnable dynamic factor is introduced into the neural Gaussian attributes to adaptively modulate rendering opacity. This formulation isolates dynamic pixels during optimization, effectively preventing moving entities from corrupting the static background. Extensive experiments on congested urban datasets demonstrate that our method significantly outperforms mainstream algorithms in suppressing dynamic vehicle artifacts. Quantitatively, our approach reduces the Artifact Pixel Density (APD) to just 0.07% in highly dynamic scenes, a substantial improvement over the 3.90% typical of baseline methods. Most importantly, the synthesized clean orthophotos achieve rigorous mapping-grade accuracy, preserving geometric precision and structural fidelity comparable to established commercial pipelines, thereby providing a robust solution for practical urban mapping.
